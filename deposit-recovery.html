FAQ

Good to know.

Can a landlord in Italy keep my deposit?
Only for documented damages beyond normal wear, or unpaid rent — itemized and proven. Under art. 1590 c.c. the deposit must otherwise be returned; silence or vague claims are not legal grounds.
How does the €99 + 20% work?
€99 covers the case assessment and the formal demand. The 20% success fee applies only to money actually recovered — if nothing comes back, you never pay it.
What do you actually do?
We assess your position, send a formal demand (PEC or registered letter) citing the correct law, negotiate with the landlord or agency, and if needed prepare the file for a partner lawyer — most cases settle before that.
I already left Italy — can you still help?
Yes. Everything happens remotely: documents by email, we act in Italy on your behalf, recovered funds arrive by bank transfer.
How long does it take?
Most cases resolve within 2–6 weeks of the formal demand.
