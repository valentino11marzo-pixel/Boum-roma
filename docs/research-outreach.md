# BOOM · Research & ERC outreach (grant-funded, recurring, high-trust)

**Why this matters:** research relocations are mid-term (2–5 years), often with
families, and **funded** — ERC and Marie-Skłodowska-Curie (MSCA) grants include
relocation/mobility allowances, and institutions hold relocation budgets. The
money exists; the pain (housing a foreign fellow on a fixed-term contract) is
real; and one welcome office or PI sends fellows every single year. Trusted,
recurring, prestigious by association.

## The pitch (problem → BOOM → proof → ask)

1. **Problem:** a brilliant fellow wins a grant, then loses weeks fighting Rome's
   rental market in a language they don't speak — landlords wary of fixed-term
   contracts, families needing schools, invoices that must be grant-clean.
2. **BOOM:** verified home, a lease that fits a fixed-term grant, deposit,
   utilities, codice fiscale, residence, schools & family settling-in — English-
   first, with a **VAT invoice that reimburses cleanly** from the grant.
3. **Proof (new brand → earn trust):** registered Italian agency (Egidi
   Immobiliare S.r.l., P.IVA 17322991005), legal contracts, 4.9★, fully
   refundable if we don't find a home.
4. **Ask:** “Can we be your go-to housing partner for incoming fellows? It's free
   to set up and we can pilot it with your next arrival.”

## Cadence & mechanics

- **3-touch:** email → 1-week follow-up with the one-page summary → LinkedIn
  connect. Time it to **recruitment season** (fellows arrive Sept–Oct & Jan–Mar).
- **The free institutional partnership is the wedge.** It costs the office
  nothing to say yes; revenue comes from the per-fellow packages they (or the
  fellows) choose.
- **Use the grant angle explicitly** — “grant-eligible, VAT-invoiced,
  reimbursable” is what makes a finance/grant office comfortable.
- **Track** via the UTM link (see docs/attribution.md).
