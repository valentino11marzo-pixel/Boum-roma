Why it feels good

The “addictive” part is restraint with reward: every action gives a precise, instant, physical-feeling response — and nothing animates that doesn’t mean something.

The rewards

  • Magnetic CTAs — gold buttons lean toward your cursor and spring back. Pure transform, <1ms.
  • Weighted toggle — the segmented slider has a spring (“thunk”) easing, like a real switch.
  • Number roll-up — costs/stats tick up once on reveal. A tiny dopamine confirm.
  • Fluted light — machined reeds catch a gold highlight that follows you.
  • Scan-line reveal — a thin gold line reads each section in as you arrive.

The guardrails

  • Transform/opacity only — GPU-cheap; no layout thrash.
  • Pointer = desktop — magnetics/tilt only where there’s a real cursor; touch gets crisp taps.
  • Reduced-motion honored — everything degrades to instant.
  • Once, not forever — reveals/roll-ups fire a single time. No idle loops eating battery.
  • Content first — never blocks reading prices or text.
